My Journey — From Engineer to Tech Writer

July 1, 2024 · 2 min read

Ever since graduating from the Amrita School of Engineering and starting my career at SAP Ariba, I was fascinated by how complex systems could be broken down into clear, usable guides and thoughtful documentation. My path to tech writing wasn't planned—it grew naturally from a desire to bridge the gap between code and people.

🎓 My Early Engineering Days​

At university, I thrived on solving technical problems. Between labs and assignments, I started writing technical posts for classmates—whether explaining networking concepts or breaking down programming assignments. That early experience taught me how to structure ideas for readability and clarity.

🔧 First Steps at Infosys/Edgeverve​

I joined Infosys right out of campus and worked on their cloud projects. In addition to coding, my work also involved contributing to FRD, BRD, or migration documents. I got an opportunity to move to the technical writing domain as part of internal job transfer. Joining Edgeverve as an Content Writer marked a crucial turning point. My role involved:

  • Writing and updating API docs.
  • Creating user-focused guides for Finacle.
  • Collaborating closely with developers and UX designers.

This wasn’t just documentation—it was about creating practical resources that help people work smarter.

🧩 Embracing “Docs as Code”​

As I refined my skills, I have started experimenting (in my personal time, of course) with “Docs as Code” approach:

  • 💾 Markdown + Git for version-controlled writing.
  • ⚙️ Docusaurus to build sleek, structured documentation sites.
  • 🤖 CI/CD pipelines to automate review and deployment workflows.

This integration of documentation into development workflows significantly increases consistency and maintains quality.

🌱 Why I Chose This Path​

I love how tech writing:

  1. Unpacks tech into human-friendly language.
  2. Keeps evolving—every release is a new story to tell.
  3. Enhances developer experience and user satisfaction.

Good docs aren’t just user-help—they’re a driver of adoption and delight.

🚀 What I’m Focused On Today​

  • Crafting clear API and user-centric documentation.
  • Creating tutorials, videos, and UX copy.
  • Contributing to docs ecosystems that scale with features.

And my goal stays constant: to make technology approachable and powerful for everyone.

If you’re curious about building docs as code or discussing technical content, I’d love to connect!
Feel free to reach out on 🔗 LinkedIn.

“Treat documentation like code—to ensure it’s versioned, reviewed, and continuously improved.”

Thanks for reading—here’s to the next chapter in clear, impactful communication!
